Listed below are some of the pioneers of first-aid history, without whom first-aid, as we know it today, would not have been possible.

Name Image Contribution
Baron Dominique-Jean Larrey Baron Dominique-Jean Larrey was Napoleon's surgeon and is credited with creating an ambulance corps. They were called the "ambulance volantes", and included medical assistants who were tasked to administer first aid to the soldiers injured in battle.
William Hawes In 1773, he began publicizing the power of artificial respiration as means of resuscitation of those who appeared drowned. This led to the formation of the Society for the Recovery of Persons Apparently Drowned in 1774, and later the Royal Humane Society.
Jean-Henri Dunant Jean-Henry Durant was a Swiss businessman who witnessed the aftermath of the Battle of Solferino in 1859. Later on, his efforts led to the formation of the Red Cross, with a key stated aim of "aid to sick and wounded soldiers in the field". The Red Cross and Red Crescent are still the largest provider of first aid worldwide.
Friedrich von Esmarch Friedrich von Esmarch was a Prussian military surgeon who, in 1870, introduced and formalized first aid to the military, and first coined the term "erste hilfe" (translating to 'first aid'). It included training for soldiers in the Franco-Prussian War on care for wounded comrades using pre-learnt bandaging and splinting skills, and making use of the Esmarch bandage designed by him.
Order of Saint John of Jerusalem In 1872, the Order of Saint John of Jerusalem in England changed its focus from hospice care and started with making a grant towards the establishment of the UK's first ambulance service. This was followed by creating its own wheeled transport litter in 1875 (the St John Ambulance), and in 1877 the St John Ambulance Association (the forerunner of modern-day St John Ambulance) was established "to train men and women for the benefit of the sick and wounded".
Surgeon-Major Peter Shepherd von Esmarch's new teaching of first aid, and Surgeon-Major Peter Shephard introduced a programme ,equivalent to that of Von Esmarch's first-aid training, for the British Army. He was the first user of "first aid for the injured" in English, and disseminated information through a series of lectures.
Colonel Francis Duncan Around in 1878, Colonel Francis Duncan took advantage of the newly charitable focus of St John, and established the concept of teaching first aid skills to civilians. The first classes were conducted in the hall of the Presbyterian school in Woolwich using a comprehensive first aid curriculum.
